Monkeys Paw

Summary

A family receives a cursed monkey’s paw that grants wishes, but each wish comes with tragic consequences.

Despite warnings, Mr. White wishes for £200, unknowingly setting fate into motion.

The paw moves, foreshadowing the horrors to come.

Then they find out that their son, Herbert died from getting stuck in machinery.

This was the consequence for the wish.

decision analysis

Mr. White’s decision to keep and use the monkey’s paw, despite warnings, shows humanity’s temptation to alter fate.

His wish for £200 seems harmless, yet the paw’s movement hints at the dangerous consequences of interfering with destiny.

The choice shows the theme—the unpredictability of wishes and the cost of greed.

Greed took the life of their son, showing the consequences of greed.

This shows that consequences can be unexpected, and can be tragic.

Evalutate

Mr. White’s decision to keep and use the monkey’s paw reflects human curiosity and disregard for warnings.

By wishing for £200, he takes a seemingly innocent action without considering the potential consequences.

The paw’s movement signals that fate has been choices suggesting that his choice may lead to regret or suffering.

When the consequence, Herbert dieing came they tried to bring him back in desperation but failed.

They didnt listen and they faced the consequences.

A wiser choice - Part 1

Instead of wishing hastily, Mr. White could have taken the sergeant-major’s warnings seriously and avoided using the monkey’s paw.

He could have recognized that interfering with fate carries unknown risks, making any wish dangerous.

By choosing to discard the paw, he would have protected his family from unforeseen consequences.

Part 2

Patience and contentment are more reliable paths to happiness than attempting to manipulate destiny.

Listening to those with experience, such as the sergeant-major, could have prevented future sorrow.

Had he chosen wisdom over temptation, he might have avoided regret and tragedy.
